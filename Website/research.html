Thesis

Honours Thesis: Intelligent Autonomous Systems for Environmental and Industrial Applications

Finn Wilson

B. Engineering Honours (Mechatronics/Space) & B. Science (Advanced Physics), Honours Class I, The University of Sydney

2024

This thesis presents novel approaches to two distinct but related challenges in autonomous systems: UAV-based bird deterrence and optimized mineral exploration. The first component introduces a magnetodynamic framework for coordinating UAV swarms to efficiently herd birds away from sensitive areas, drawing inspiration from natural predator-prey dynamics. The second component develops machine learning methods for mineral exploration that significantly reduce sampling costs while maintaining high prediction accuracy.

Underwater Change Detection in Variable Conditions Using Neural Networks

Finn Wilson, Co-Author 3, Co-Author 4

Australian Conference on Robotics and Automation (ACRA)

2023

This paper presents a novel approach to underwater change detection using neural networks specifically designed to overcome challenges from variable water conditions, lighting, and lack of rigid structures. The method achieves robust detection performance in environments where traditional computer vision techniques struggle, offering potential applications in marine conservation, underwater infrastructure monitoring, and autonomous underwater vehicle navigation.

Method and System for Optimizing Geological Sampling Using Adaptive Machine Learning

Co-Author 1, Finn Wilson, Co-Author 2, Rio Tinto Innovation

Status: Patent Application Filed

2024

This patent application covers novel methods for determining optimal sampling locations in geological exploration using adaptive machine learning techniques. The system iteratively recommends sampling locations to maximize information gain while minimizing exploration costs, leveraging Gaussian Processes and ensemble learning to quantify uncertainty and guide decision-making in resource exploration campaigns.
